The Group's Reflections

Emre's

What went well?

The group was able to delegate tasks quickly and efficiently. No one had any problems with the tasks they were given and completed them in a timely manner.

What could be improved?

Using the chat in Teams efficiently. Quite a few times we went off topic. Next time we should perhaps create two separate chats, one for on topic discussions and another for general talk.

At least one thing that was surprising?

The most surprising thing would have to be how well we got along. We have a quite diverse group with people from multiple backgrounds, yet everyone was able to work as a team despite the age differences and cultural differences.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups?

I’ve learnt how we as a group can utilize all our different skills and combine our efforts to produce a great result.

Github Activity?

Our Github log of activity was a great effort for a first try. Personally, my activity looked like it is a bit all over the place since I was still getting used to using Github and forking/merging. However as a team I believe we did quite well in keeping track all of the changes committed and recording them effectively.

Andrews's

What went well?

In this group assignment what went fully well for the team members was the rate in which the task was assigned to each team member and how everyone understood the task that they needed to complete. Everything was done with consistency, coherently and effectively as possible.

What could be improved?

Nothing needed to be improved in the chats, seeing that it was mainly one monolithic chat called “general” which does not imply work. Instead what should have been set up was two chats, one that showed the title to describe nicely the intent of the chat for work. Another chat on the other had for off work topics. This is something commonly associated with Discord but should have been incorporated in Microsoft teams.

At least one thing that was surprising?

What was surprising was how the team meetings went for when we had gathered for our team meetings and how everyone went about working on the project, while collaborating in the group. We all come from different walks of life, but still manage to pull put all the pieces of the assignment together.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups?

What was learned when working together as a team was a bit the team members backgrounds and what they aspire to be in the future and their interest. I also learned how the team worked well together, because sometimes out there in an academic context or in the real world, there are teams that do not work well with each other.

Github Activity?

We tried our best and it worked well on how GitHub. For me personally I have experience using a bit of GitHub, but I never decided out of my own terms not to use it personally yet outside of the team project. As a team to explain more precisely what worked well is to say that all commits, and changes made to the commit were recorded in an efficient manner.
